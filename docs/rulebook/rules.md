# Volcano Rush - Rulebook

A semi-cooperative survival game for 6 to 8 players. Play time: 30 to 60 minutes.

This rulebook is everything you need to learn and play the game. Read it once from start to finish before your first session, then keep it next to the table for reference.

## The story

You and your group are stranded on the slope of an active volcano. The mountain rumbles, and it will not be quiet for long. Working together, you must gather resources, prepare food, build shelter, and assemble a boat. Escape together before the **Eruption** card is drawn, or no one leaves the island.

The group wins or loses as a whole. But once the boat is built, the player who contributed the most is remembered as the hero of the journey.

---

## Goal of the game

Build the required number of **boat parts** before the **Eruption** volcano card is revealed.

| Players | Boat parts required |
|---|---|
| 6 | 3 (Keel, Hull, Mast) |
| 7 | 4 (Keel, Hull, Mast, Sail) |
| 8 | 5 (Keel, Hull, Mast, Sail, Rudder) |

If you finish the boat in time, the whole group wins. If the Eruption card is revealed first, the whole group loses.

## Components

| Component | Count | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Resource cards | 60 | 20 Wood, 20 Stone, 20 Rope |
| Character cards | 6 | Builder, Fire Starter, Craftsman, Cook, Gatherer, Sailor |
| Mission cards | 13 | 8 standard missions plus 5 boat parts |
| Complication cards | 11 | Drawn during every mission |
| Volcano cards | 11 | Includes the **Eruption** card |
| Tool tokens | 2 | Knife and Vessel (shared by the group) |

When the resource deck or the complication deck runs out, reshuffle the discards and keep playing.

## Setup

1. **Choose roles.** Shuffle the character cards according to the table below and deal one face up to each player.

   | Players | Character distribution |
   |---|---|
   | 6 | All six characters, one each |
   | 7 | All six characters, plus one extra (any character except Craftsman, chosen at random) |
   | 8 | All six characters, plus two extras (any characters except Craftsman, chosen at random) |

2. **Deal resources.** Shuffle the resource deck. Deal **3 cards** face down to each player. Players keep their hand visible to themselves only.

3. **Reveal active missions.** Shuffle the mission deck. For 6 players, remove **Make the Sail** and **Fit the Rudder** before shuffling. For 7 players, remove only **Fit the Rudder**. For 8 players, use all 13 missions. Reveal the top **3 mission cards** to form the active mission pool.

4. **Place the tools.** Set the **Knife** and **Vessel** tokens in the centre of the table, both undamaged.

5. **Prepare the volcano deck.** Take the **Eruption** card and place it at the bottom of the volcano discard area. Shuffle the other 10 volcano cards into a face-down deck on top of it. The Eruption card will only ever come out after every other volcano card has been drawn.

6. **Choose the first active player.** Pick any player to start. The active player role rotates clockwise after every round.

You are ready to play.

## How a round works

Each round has one **active player** who makes the key decisions for the round. Run the round in this order:

### Step 1. Begin the round

- Any tool that finished its repair this round becomes undamaged again.
- Any player whose exhaustion has run out becomes available again.

### Step 2. Active player chooses an action

The active player chooses **one** of:

- **Pick a mission.** Choose 1 of the 3 active missions to attempt this round.
- **Shuffle the mission pool.** Discard 1 resource card from the active player's hand (any type), then shuffle every face-down mission back into the deck and reveal 3 new active missions. After shuffling, draw 1 volcano card. The round then ends. The active player cannot shuffle if their hand is empty.

If no mission can be legally attempted (for example, the volcano card **Panic** is in effect and only boat missions are available, and the active player has no resource to spend on a shuffle), the round is **forfeited**: draw 1 volcano card and end the round.

### Step 3. Choose participants

When a mission is picked, the active player names the participants. Each mission lists how many participants it needs. The active player may include themselves or not.

A player **cannot participate** if they are currently Exhausted.

### Step 4. Non-participants act

Every player who is not a participant chooses one of the following:

- **Gather** (default). At the end of the round, draw 1 resource card from the deck. The Gatherer character can choose to draw 3 instead (see [Characters](#characters)).
- **Repair a tool** (Craftsman only). Pay 1 Stone from your hand to start repairing one damaged tool. See [Tools and repair](#tools-and-repair).

### Step 5. Draw a complication card

Shuffle the complication deck and draw 1 card. Apply its effect to this mission only. If a Sailor is among the participants and the mission is a boat mission, draw 2 complication cards instead and choose which one applies.

### Step 6. Resolve the mission

See [Resolving a mission](#resolving-a-mission) for the full procedure. The mission either succeeds or fails.

- **On success**, every participant scores the mission's points, the mission's bonus effect triggers, and the mission card is removed from the pool.
- **On failure**, the mission stays in the active pool, and 1 volcano card is drawn (unless a previous mission's bonus protects the group from this draw).

### Step 7. Apply exhaustion

Every participant becomes **Exhausted** and may not participate in a mission next round. (See [Exhaustion](#exhaustion).)

### Step 8. Resolve gather actions

Every non-participant who chose Gather draws their resource cards now.

### Step 9. End the round

- If the mission succeeded, replace it by revealing a new mission card so the active pool is back to 3.
- The active player role passes one seat clockwise.
- Check the win condition: if every required boat part has been built, the group wins immediately.

## Resolving a mission

Resources are paid **per participant**, never pooled. Each participant pays their own share from their own hand. If even one participant cannot cover their share, the mission fails.

For each participant, calculate the resources they owe in this order:

1. **Base mission cost.** Take the mission's per-player resource list.
2. **Apply the participant's character discount.** For example, a Builder on a boat or shelter mission pays 1 less Wood; a Fire Starter on a fire mission pays 1 less of any one resource.
3. **Add complication extras.** If the complication card adds resources (for example, "+1 Rope per participant"), each participant adds that to their share. Complications that add "any" resource let each participant choose which type they pay.
4. **Add volcano extras.** If a volcano card such as **Rain and Mud** or **Lava Flow** is currently active, each participant adds those extras too.

If every participant's hand can cover their share, the mission **succeeds**. Each participant discards the resources they paid.

A mission also fails immediately if it requires a tool (Knife or Vessel) and that tool is currently damaged.

### Worked example

Three participants attempt **Cut the Keel** (1 Wood plus 1 Rope, per participant). The complication is **Mosquito Attack** (+1 Rope). The volcano card **Rain and Mud** is in effect.

Each participant owes: 1 Wood (base) + 1 Rope (base) + 1 Rope (complication) + 2 Wood (Rain and Mud, because the mission requires Wood) = **3 Wood and 2 Rope**.

A Builder participant subtracts 1 Wood from their share, owing **2 Wood and 2 Rope** instead.

If every participant can pay their own share from their own hand, the mission succeeds and the Keel is built.

## Characters

Each character has one passive or active ability. Abilities only trigger in the situations described.

### Builder
On any **Shelter** or **Boat** mission that requires Wood, the Builder pays 1 less Wood. No effect on Fire or Food missions.

### Fire Starter
On any **Fire** mission, the Fire Starter pays 1 less of any one resource. On a successful Fire mission, every participant gets +1 bonus point.

### Craftsman
Instead of joining a mission, a Craftsman who is not Exhausted may **repair 1 damaged tool** by paying 1 Stone. The Craftsman scores +1 point immediately for starting the repair. Only one tool can be under repair at a time.

### Cook
On a successful **Food** mission, every participant gets +1 bonus point.

### Gatherer
When the Gatherer chooses **Gather**, they may draw **3 resource cards** instead of 1. If they do, they become Exhausted afterwards. They may not use this ability while already Exhausted.

### Sailor
When the Sailor participates in a **Boat** mission, draw **2 complication cards** and choose which one applies.

## Missions

There are 13 missions in total: 8 standard and 5 boat parts. The active pool always shows 3 face-up missions until the boat is complete.

### Standard missions

| Mission | Type | Per player | Tools | Players | Points | Bonus on success |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Light a Fire** | Fire | 1 Wood | - | 2 | 2 | Each participant immediately draws 1 resource card |
| **Torch for the Night** | Fire | 1 Wood, 1 Rope | - | 3 | 3 | Skip the next complication card draw (one-time) |
| **Fetch Water** | Food | 1 Rope, 1 Wood | Vessel | 3 | 3 | All players who Gather next round draw 1 extra resource |
| **Hunt** | Food | 1 Stone, 1 Rope | Knife | 3 | 3 | If the next mission fails, no volcano card is drawn (one-time) |
| **Prepare Food** | Food | 1 Wood | Knife, Vessel | 3 | 3 | Participants in this mission are not Exhausted afterwards |
| **Build a Shelter** | Shelter | 1 Wood, 1 Stone | Knife | 4 | 4 | Negate the volcano card Rain and Mud (one-time) |
| **Fortify the Camp** | Shelter | 1 Rope | Knife | 2 | 2 | Every player who currently has 0 resources immediately draws 1 resource card |
| **Gather Materials** | Shelter | 1 Wood, 1 Stone, 1 Rope | - | 3 | 3 | - |

### Boat missions

These are the missions that build the boat. Boat missions must be **completed in order**: Keel, then Hull, then Mast, then Sail, then Rudder. A later boat part cannot be attempted before the earlier ones are built.

| Mission | Boat part | Per player | Tools | Players | Points | Used at |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Cut the Keel** | Keel | 1 Wood, 1 Rope | Knife | 3 | 1 | 6, 7, 8 players |
| **Assemble the Hull** | Hull | 1 Wood, 1 Stone | - | 3 | 1 | 6, 7, 8 players |
| **Raise the Mast** | Mast | 1 Wood, 1 Rope | Vessel | 3 | 1 | 6, 7, 8 players |
| **Make the Sail** | Sail | 1 Wood, 1 Rope | - | 4 | 1 | 7, 8 players only |
| **Fit the Rudder** | Rudder | 1 Wood, 1 Rope | Vessel | 4 | 1 | 8 players only |

## Complication cards

After participants are declared, draw 1 complication card and apply it to the mission. **Resource extras are paid per participant**, the same as the base mission cost.

| Card | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Mosquito Attack** | +1 Rope per participant |
| **Wet Wood** | +1 Wood per participant, only if the mission requires Wood |
| **Collapsed Path** | +1 Stone per participant |
| **Slippery Rocks** | +2 resources per participant (any combination) |
| **Blunt Blade** | If the mission succeeds, the Knife becomes damaged afterwards |
| **Cracked Vessel** | If the mission succeeds, the Vessel becomes damaged afterwards |
| **Heat and Thirst** | +1 resource per participant (any type) |
| **Night Anxiety** | The mission needs 1 extra helper from outside the participants. The helper contributes resources but earns no points. If no one is willing or able to help, the mission fails |
| **Camp Panic** | Each participant may contribute at most 1 card of any single resource type |
| **Calm Breeze** | No effect |
| **Clear Sky** | No effect |

When the complication deck runs out, reshuffle the discards and keep playing.

## Volcano cards

A volcano card is drawn whenever a mission **fails** (and the group is not protected by **Hunt**'s bonus), or whenever the active player **shuffles** the mission pool, or the round is forfeited. Some volcano cards take effect immediately; others are pending and apply to the next mission.

The **Eruption** card sits at the bottom of the deck. The moment it is drawn, the group loses.

| Card | When it triggers | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Rain and Mud** | Pending until the next mission | The next mission requires +2 Wood per participant, only if the mission requires Wood |
| **Ash in the Air** | Pending until the next mission | Participants of the next mission stay Exhausted for 2 rounds instead of 1 |
| **Tremor** | Immediate | Discard 1 non-boat active mission and reveal a new one. If all active missions are boat parts, no effect |
| **Storm** | Immediate | Each player loses 1 random resource from their hand (if they have any) |
| **Lava Flow** | Pending until the next mission | The next mission requires +1 Rope per participant |
| **Panic** | Pending until the end of the next round | Boat missions cannot be chosen next round |
| **Collapse** | Immediate | Every player with 3 or more resources loses 1 random resource |
| **Heat Wave** | Pending until the end of the next round | Players who Gather next round draw 0 resources |
| **Smoke** | Pending until the next mission | The next mission gives 1 fewer point to each participant (minimum 0) |
| **Earthquake** | Immediate | Every currently Exhausted player remains Exhausted for 1 additional round |
| **Eruption** | Immediate | Game over: the group loses |

## Tools and repair

The group shares two tools: the **Knife** and the **Vessel**. Some missions require a tool. If the tool is damaged at the moment a mission is attempted, the mission fails immediately, regardless of resources.

A tool becomes damaged when a complication card (**Blunt Blade** or **Cracked Vessel**) damages it on a successful mission.

A damaged tool is repaired in one of two ways:

- A **Craftsman** spends 1 Stone during the non-participant action step to begin a repair. The tool is ready again at the start of the next round. The Craftsman scores +1 point for starting the repair.
- The **Gather Materials** mission's bonus repairs 1 damaged tool immediately on success.

Only one tool can be under repair at a time.

---

## Exhaustion

A participant in a mission becomes **Exhausted** afterwards. Exhausted players may not participate in the next mission. They can still gather, and a Craftsman cannot start a repair while Exhausted.

Exhaustion normally lasts until the end of the next round, after which the player is available again.

These effects change exhaustion:

- **Prepare Food** bonus: participants in that mission are **not** Exhausted afterwards.
- **Ash in the Air** volcano card: participants of the next mission stay Exhausted for 2 rounds instead of 1.
- **Earthquake** volcano card: every currently Exhausted player stays Exhausted for 1 additional round.
- **Gatherer** drawing 3 resources: the Gatherer becomes Exhausted afterwards.

---

## Winning and losing

**The group wins** when the last required boat part is built. (3 parts at 6 players, 4 at 7, 5 at 8.)

**The group loses** when the **Eruption** volcano card is revealed.

---

## Scoring

Points are only counted if the group wins. If the group loses, no one scores.

Each successful mission awards its listed points to every participant. On top of that:

- **Fire Starter** participants on a successful Fire mission give every participant +1 point.
- **Cook** participants on a successful Food mission give every participant +1 point.
- The **Craftsman** scores +1 point each time they start a repair.

The player with the most points is the **hero of the journey**. If two or more players are tied, the player with the most unused resources in hand wins the tiebreaker.

## Quick reference

**Round phases**
1. Begin round (repairs finish, exhaustion clears)
2. Active player picks a mission or shuffles
3. Choose participants
4. Non-participants choose to repair or gather
5. Draw complication
6. Resolve mission
7. Apply exhaustion to participants
8. Non-participants gather
9. End round (replace mission, rotate active player, check win)

**Always per participant.** Base cost, character discount, complication extras, volcano extras, pending bonuses: all calculated for each participant individually from their own hand.

**Shuffle cost.** 1 random resource from the active player plus 1 volcano draw.

**Tool repair.** Craftsman spends 1 Stone, takes 1 round to finish, only one tool at a time.

**Exhaustion.** Lasts 1 round (the round after the mission), unless modified by Ash, Earthquake, or Prepare Food.

**Win.** All required boat parts built.

**Lose.** Eruption volcano card revealed.

## Open rules questions

These are points where the existing reference documents and the simulation engine disagree, or where the rules need a clear authorial decision before the rulebook can be considered final. The behaviour described in the sections above follows the existing reference documents; the engine's behaviour is noted here for resolution.

- **Volcano extras: per participant or pooled?** This rulebook and `complication_cards.md` say volcano extras (Rain and Mud, Lava Flow) are paid per participant. The engine agrees. The original `game_rules.md` says they are "a one-time group cost paid from the participants' pooled surplus." The pooled wording in `game_rules.md` should be removed.
- **Hunt's protection vs. Eruption.** Hunt's "no volcano card is drawn next failure" bonus protects against any volcano draw, including Eruption, in the engine. Decide whether the boxed game should let Hunt cancel an Eruption draw or treat Eruption as unstoppable.
- **Stacking pending volcano effects.** What happens when two pending volcano effects could both apply to the next mission (for example **Smoke** then **Lava Flow** drawn on consecutive failures)? The rulebook does not currently say whether they queue, override, or both apply. Clarify the rule.
- **Ash plus Earthquake stacking.** Ash extends the next mission's exhaustion to 2 rounds. Earthquake extends every active exhaustion by 1 more round. If both are in play, do they stack additively (3 rounds) or cap somewhere?
- **Storm and Collapse losses.** "Lose 1 random resource" applies to a player's resource hand. Confirm tools are never affected, even if a future card edit references a "random card."
- **Boat construction order enforcement.** The mission deck and player-count rules already restrict which boat missions are revealed. Confirm that within an active pool the boat parts must still be attempted in Keel - Hull - Mast - Sail - Rudder order, and not whichever boat part the active player prefers to try first.
- **Active player turn-end after a successful mission.** After a successful mission the active player still passes the role clockwise. Confirm there is no extra turn or reward mechanic intended for the active player who chose a winning mission.
- **Sailor Complication choice.** The Sailor draws 2 cards and chooses which one applies on a Boat mission. Confirm whether the Sailor's choice is private or visible, and whether the unchosen card is discarded or returned to the deck.
- **Night Anxiety helper resources.** A Night Anxiety helper "contributes resources but earns no points." Decide whether the helper is also charged the per-participant complication and volcano extras, or only the base resource shortfall they were called to cover.

When these are resolved, update both this rulebook and the corresponding source files in `docs/` and `simulation_engine/`.
